Radiator Size Guide: How to Measure and Size Your Radiator

If you’re looking to upgrade or replace your radiator, knowing its size is crucial to ensure a smooth installation. In this guide, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know about how to size radiators, and correctly measure the dimensions of a central heating radiator – the kind that’s plugged directly into the boiler and heating supply.

How to Measure Your Radiator

To make sure your new radiator fits your plumbing and location specifications, you’ll need to take accurate measurements of your current radiator. Here are the steps you need to follow:

  1. Prepare the right tools: You’ll need a tape measure, pen and notepad, or your phone to record the measurements.
  2. Measure the radiator height: Measure from the lowest point of your radiator to the highest.
  3. Measure the radiator width: Measure from the furthest point on the left to the furthest point on the right of your radiator.

  5. Measure the pipe center to center distance: This is the distance between the two pipe inlets that feed into, and out of, your radiator.
  6. Measure the wall surface to the pipes’ centers: Begin at the wall and measure the short gap between the wall to the center of the pipes.
  7. Measure the depth of your current radiator: If it sticks out, you might consider replacing it with a slimline version.
  8. Measure the space behind your radiator: Measure the distance between the wall and the radiator.

All measurements should be taken in millimeters.

What Size Radiator Do You Need?

Radiators come in various sizes and shapes to cater to different heating requirements and preferences. To calculate the ideal size of your radiator, you need to determine the British Thermal Units (BTU) required for your room. The BTU calculation takes into account the size and type of the room, such as a kitchen, bedroom, or common areas, and is usually expressed as a multiplication factor of the cubic feet of the room. Double glazing is also factored in by deducting 10% from the BTU calculation.

Radiator Tips

When buying a new radiator, it’s advisable to match its measurements precisely with the old one, unless you’re willing to make changes to your plumbing. You can research and compare different radiator models and deals online before visiting a showroom to see how the radiator sizing might appear in your home.
